Academic Experience at the University of Georgia:

During my time at the University of Georgia, I completed a Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering, engaging in a curriculum designed to build both technical expertise and problem-solving abilities. My coursework spanned fundamental sciences, core engineering principles, and advanced design topics, including:

Thermodynamics & Heat Transfer – Gained a deep understanding of energy systems, efficiency analysis, and thermal management strategies used in mechanical and HVAC design.

Fluid Mechanics & Hydraulics – Learned to analyze fluid behavior in systems, enabling applications in piping, pump design, and water system engineering.

Engineering Mechanics (Statics & Dynamics) – Developed a solid foundation in force analysis, motion, and structural stability for mechanical and structural systems.

Materials Science – Studied the properties, selection, and processing of materials, critical for product design and manufacturing decisions.

Mechanical Design & CAD Modeling – Applied design principles using tools such as AutoCAD to create accurate technical drawings and prototypes.

Manufacturing and Design – Learned about fabrication techniques, CNC machining, additive manufacturing, and quality control principles.

Capstone Design Project – Led a sustainable water system project in partnership with Terrapin Beer Company, integrating teamwork, engineering design, and project management.

Through these courses, I built skills in problem-solving, technical communication, data analysis, and cross-disciplinary collaboration. My experience prepared me to approach engineering challenges with a balance of creativity, analytical thinking, and practical application.
